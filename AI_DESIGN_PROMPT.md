# Maatouk Studio — Design Brief for AI Tools

**Paste this entire prompt into your chosen AI design tool** (Lovable, v0.dev, Google Stitch, Figma Make, etc.)

---

## THE PROJECT

Design a full marketing website for **Maatouk Studio** — a Gulf-based creative studio specializing in three disciplines: **Branding**, **Motion Design**, and **Digital Products** (websites, e-commerce, ERP systems).

The studio is **bilingual by default** — every page must support **Arabic (RTL) and English (LTR)** with equal typographic care. Arabic is the primary market.

---

## BRAND GUIDELINES (STRICT — do not change)

- **Studio name:** Maatouk Studio (EN) / ستوديو معتوق (AR)
- **Primary color:** `#0029D6` (deep electric blue)
- **Accent color:** `#3CFFC5` (mint green)
- **Surface color:** `#FBF9F9` (warm off-white)
- **Text color:** `#1B1B1C` (near black)
- **Display font:** Lyon (serif, for Latin headlines) / Use a similar editorial serif
- **Arabic font:** Mizan (Arabic serif) / Use a refined Arabic serif like Noto Naskh Arabic or IBM Plex Sans Arabic
- **Logo:** A geometric diamond mark made of 4 rotated squares forming an X pattern

---

## DESIGN PERSONALITY

**MUST feel like:**
- High-end editorial magazine (think *Kinfolk*, *Monocle*, *Wallpaper***)
- Pentagram, Koto Studio, Made Thought, Instrument — top-tier creative studios
- Gallery-like: generous whitespace, considered typography, bold scale
- Arabic-first — Arabic typography as beautiful as Latin

**MUST NOT feel like:**
- Silicon Valley SaaS dashboard
- Generic "tech startup" template
- Heavy animations, floating gradient blobs
- Stock photography or decorative illustrations

## CONTENT TONE

- Confident but not arrogant
- Editorial — sentences that read like a magazine
- Bilingual with dignity — Arabic should not be translated literally from English, it should read as original Arabic
- No buzzwords ("disrupt", "synergy", "paradigm")
- No emoji
- Numbers in Arabic sections use Arabic-Indic numerals where appropriate (٢٠٢٦)

---

## LAYOUT PREFERENCES

- **Typography-forward** — typography IS the design
- **Extreme scale play** — headlines at 6-10rem, body at 16-18px, no medium sizes
- **Wide margins** — content breathes
- **Asymmetric where it adds clarity** — not decoration
- **Minimal borders/boxes** — rely on typography and space
- **Numbered sections** (§01, §02) as editorial devices

---

## MOTION & INTERACTION

- Subtle, considered motion only
- No bouncing, no gradient shifts
- Typography reveals, not "cards flying in"
- Smooth scroll with section transitions
- Hover states that feel intentional
